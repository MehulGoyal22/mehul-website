Current Projects

Polarization in Accretion Disks

Polarisation measurements can offer valuable insights into the geometry and structure of accretion disks around compact objects. I am working under the guidance of Prof. Alexandra Veledina to explore the relativistic effects on the polarisation properties of emission from accretion disks around black holes. This project involves studying analytical calculations and performing numerical simulations to understand how the polarisation signal is affected by relativistic effects such as Doppler boosting and light-bending.

Kilonova Lightcurves Inference using Machine Learning

Kilonovae are transient astronomical events resulting from the merger of neutron stars or a neutron star and a black hole. I am working with Prof. Michael Coughlin and Malina Desai on testing and optimising machine learning models to infer the properties of kilonova lightcurves. This project involves using synthetic data generated from numerical simulations to train and validate machine learning models, with the aim to extract physical parameters such as ejecta mass, velocity, and composition from observed lightcurves.

Post-GRB Afterglow Modeling and Bayesian Inference

Gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) are among the most energetic events in the universe, and their afterglows can provide valuable information about their progenitors. I have worked with Prof. Varun Bhalerao to use Bayesian Inference techniques to model the afterglow of GRBs. I developed code implementing MCMC and dynamic nested sampling for parameter inference of GRB afterglow lightcurves. I also studied the analytical models of afterglow emission and their implications for the physical properties of GRBs.
